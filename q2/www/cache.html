How Does Cache Work?

Why clearing your cookies resets the MailChimp form

Cache is simply hardware or software that stores data temporarily. The RAM in your computer is a form of Cache. Browser Cache is simply data that is downloaded on your computer that relates to the specific website you are visiting. When you first visit the website, it leaves a cookie in your cache that the form has popped up, so that the next time you visit, it knows not to pop up the form again. So when you delete the cache, the browser sends the request for everything since it does not have any data with the webpage and thus loads the form again.
